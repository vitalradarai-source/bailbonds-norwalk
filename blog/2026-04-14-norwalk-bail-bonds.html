Where Do Norwalk Arrests Go?

When Los Angeles County Sheriff's Department (LASD) Norwalk Station deputies make an arrest within their jurisdiction, the person is transported to LASD Norwalk Station at 12335 Civic Center Drive, Norwalk, CA 90650 (phone: 562-863-8711). This facility is the primary booking and short-term holding location for adults arrested in Norwalk and the immediate surrounding area.

The Norwalk Station is a relatively small holding facility — it is designed for temporary custody, not long-term housing. Once booking is complete, several things can happen depending on the charges and circumstances:

  • Misdemeanor charges: The defendant may be released on their own recognizance (OR) or held pending bail at Norwalk Station itself. Many misdemeanor defendants are released within hours of posting a bond at this location.
  • Felony charges: Defendants booked on felony charges are typically transferred to a larger Los Angeles County facility — most commonly Twin Towers Correctional Facility (450 Bauchet St, Los Angeles) or Men's Central Jail (441 Bauchet St, Los Angeles). The transfer usually occurs within 12 to 24 hours of initial booking.
  • Outstanding warrants or holds: If the defendant has an active warrant from another jurisdiction, or a hold from another agency, they may be transferred to a facility that handles those cases. We track these transfers and post bonds at whichever location holds your family member.

Once in LASD custody, the defendant's booking information appears in the LASD Inmate Information Center (app5.lasd.org). This database can take 2 to 6 hours to update after the actual booking. Angels Bail Bonds has direct contacts within the system and can often confirm location and bail amount before the public record reflects it.

The Norwalk Courthouse — Why It Matters for Bail

Here is a detail that sets Norwalk apart from most other cities served by Angels Bail Bonds: Norwalk is home to the main hub of the Los Angeles County Superior Court's Southeast District.

The Norwalk Courthouse at 12720 Norwalk Blvd, Norwalk, CA 90650 is the administrative and judicial center of the Southeast District — one of the busiest regional court hubs in Los Angeles County. The Southeast District also includes branch courthouses in:

  • Downey Courthouse — serving Bell, Bell Gardens, Downey, La Mirada, Norwalk, and South Gate
  • Whittier Courthouse — serving La Habra Heights, Pico Rivera, Santa Fe Springs, and Whittier

What this means practically: if someone is arrested in Norwalk or any of the surrounding service area cities, their arraignment and all subsequent criminal hearings will take place within the Southeast District. Most arraignments for Norwalk arrests are calendared at the Norwalk Courthouse itself.

Under California Penal Code § 825, arraignment must occur within 48 hours of arrest (excluding Sundays and court holidays). This is critical: posting bail before arraignment locks in the bail schedule amount set at booking. Waiting until the arraignment date gives the judge the opportunity to raise bail, add conditions, or in serious cases, deny bail entirely under CA Constitution Article I, Section 12.

The Booking Process at LASD Norwalk Station — Step by Step

The booking process at LASD Norwalk Station follows California law. Here is exactly what happens from the moment of arrest to the point where bail can be posted:

Arrival
Transport and Property Inventory
Deputies transport the defendant to 12335 Civic Center Dr. Personal belongings — phone, wallet, keys, jewelry — are inventoried, bagged, and logged. The defendant receives a receipt for their property upon release.
Identity
ID Check and Warrant Search
Officers confirm the defendant's identity and run their name and date of birth through state and federal databases. Outstanding warrants from other jurisdictions are flagged here. An active warrant from another county or state can place a hold that complicates release even after bail is posted locally.
Biometrics
Fingerprints and Photo
Electronic fingerprints (Live Scan) are submitted to the California Department of Justice and the FBI. A booking photo is taken. Criminal history confirmed through this process can affect whether bail is set at schedule amount or whether the arresting officer flags the case for a higher bail hearing.
Medical
Health Screening
California law requires a health and mental health screening for every booked individual. Defendants with immediate medical needs may be transported to a hospital before booking is completed — this can extend the timeline before bail can be posted.
Bail Set
Bail Amount Assigned
Bail is set according to the Los Angeles County bail schedule for the charges filed. This is the amount a bondsman will work from. Bail can be posted as soon as this step is complete — California does not require any mandatory waiting period after booking for most charges.
Transfer
Transfer Decision (Felonies Only)
For felony charges, LASD will decide whether to hold the defendant at Norwalk Station or transfer to Twin Towers or Men's Central Jail. We monitor this decision and post the bond at whichever facility receives the defendant — no additional delay on our end.

Misdemeanor bookings at LASD Norwalk Station typically take 2 to 6 hours from arrival. Felony bookings can run 8 to 24 hours if a transfer to downtown LA is involved. The moment the booking is complete and bail is assigned, you can post a bail bond — no waiting period required.

How Norwalk Bail Bonds Work — The 10% Fee Explained

Most families facing a Norwalk arrest cannot pay the full bail amount in cash. That is what a bail bondsman is for. Here is the exact process, step by step:

  1. You call (626) 478-1062. We locate the defendant and confirm the bail amount from the Los Angeles County bail schedule.
  2. You pay Angels Bail Bonds a 10% premium — our fee for posting the bond. Under California Insurance Code § 1800.4, this is the state-regulated rate for all licensed bail agents in California. No bondsman can legally charge more or less than 10% on a standard bond.
  3. Angels Bail Bonds posts the full bail amount with the jail as a surety bond — a guarantee to the court that the defendant will appear at all hearings, starting with the Norwalk Courthouse arraignment.
  4. The jail processes the bond and the defendant is released — typically within 1 to 4 hours at Norwalk Station, and 4 to 12 hours if they have been transferred to Twin Towers or Men's Central Jail.
  5. The defendant attends all court dates. When the case concludes, the bond is exonerated and no further fees are owed.

The 10% premium is a fee for services rendered — it is not refunded at the end of the case. This is how every licensed California bail bondsman operates under state law. Common Norwalk Charges and What Bail Looks Like

Norwalk is a dense, working-class city of approximately 103,000 residents in the southeast corner of Los Angeles County. The city's demographics, location near major freeways (I-5, SR-91, I-605), and economic pressures produce a distinct mix of arrest charges. The most common scenarios that result in a call to a Norwalk bail bondsman:

Domestic Violence — PC 273.5 and PC 243(e)(1)

Domestic violence arrests are among the most frequent and most urgent cases we handle in Norwalk. When a DV call comes in to LASD Norwalk Station, deputies are required to make an arrest if there is probable cause to believe a crime occurred — even if the alleged victim does not want to press charges. This means arrests happen in situations families did not expect.

Penal Code 273.5 covers corporal injury on a spouse or cohabitant — any visible injury, even minor. Bail is set at $50,000 on the LA County schedule. Penal Code 243(e)(1) covers battery on a cohabitant without visible injury — bail is typically $10,000.

In nearly all Norwalk DV arrests, the court will issue a Criminal Protective Order (CPO) under Penal Code 136.2 at or before arraignment. This order restricts contact between the defendant and the protected party. It is not a bail hold — the defendant can be released on a bail bond while the CPO is in effect. The CPO governs where the defendant can go and who they can contact, not whether they can be released from custody.

There is no mandatory 72-hour hold in California DV law. That is a common misconception. Bail can be posted immediately after booking once the bail amount is assigned from the Los Angeles County schedule.

DUI — Vehicle Code 23152

DUI arrests are frequent along the I-5, SR-91, and I-605 corridors that run through and near Norwalk. A first-offense DUI under Vehicle Code 23152 carries bail in the $5,000 to $10,000 range on the LA County schedule. DUI with injury (VC 23153) is substantially higher — $100,000. Most first-offense DUI defendants in Norwalk are eligible for release on a bail bond or own recognizance within hours of booking, assuming no prior DUI record or outstanding warrants.

Drug Charges — HS 11350 / 11351 / 11377 / 11378

Simple possession charges under Health and Safety Code 11350 (controlled substances) and HS 11377 (methamphetamine) carry bail in the $10,000 to $20,000 range. Possession for sale under HS 11351 and HS 11378 jumps to $30,000. Drug arrests near Norwalk's commercial corridors and freeway off-ramps are processed at LASD Norwalk Station before potential transfer to Twin Towers.

Criminal Threats and Stalking — PC 422 and PC 646.9

Penal Code 422 (criminal threats) is often charged alongside DV arrests when threats were made during the incident. Bail is set at $50,000. Penal Code 646.9 (stalking) carries significantly higher bail — $150,000 — reflecting the serious nature of the charge. Both charges are felonies and will result in transfer to Twin Towers for most defendants. Early contact with a bondsman is especially important in these cases because the transfer window is unpredictable.

Assault with a Deadly Weapon — PC 245(a)(1)

Penal Code 245(a)(1) covers assault with a deadly weapon — a felony that carries $30,000 bail on the LA County schedule. These cases almost always result in transfer to a downtown LA county facility. When the defendant is at Twin Towers or Men's Central Jail, the bond must be posted at that facility — not at Norwalk Station. We monitor transfers and post at whichever facility holds the defendant.

What does a PC 136.2 protective order mean after a Norwalk DV arrest?

A criminal protective order under Penal Code 136.2 is a court order issued to protect an alleged victim — it is not a bail hold and does not prevent release on a bail bond. The defendant can be released on bond even with a PC 136.2 order in place. The order restricts contact with the protected party, but it has no bearing on whether bail can be posted.
